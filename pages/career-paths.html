Career Paths

Fashion tech is still young enough that there's no single right way in. But there are patterns.

Here are five real paths people take, with honest timelines, actual companies, and salary ranges that reflect what's out there in 2025-2026.

The arrows between Product, Digital Design, and Data are real -- people hop between these constantly. The ML/AI path is more specialized and harder to enter laterally, but Data & Analytics is the most common on-ramp to it. Sustainability people often come from Product or Analytics backgrounds.

Path 1: Technical (ML/AI in Fashion)

This is the highest-paying path and the hardest to break into. You're building the recommendation engines, computer vision models, virtual try-on systems, and demand forecasting tools that power modern fashion commerce.

Year 0-1: Getting Your Foot In

Titles: Junior ML Engineer, Data Analyst (ML team), ML Intern, Applied Scientist I

What you're actually doing:

  • Cleaning datasets (so much cleaning)
  • Building basic models for product categorization or size recommendation
  • Running A/B tests on recommendation features
  • Writing data pipelines, maybe some feature engineering
  • Sitting in meetings where senior engineers explain why your model won't scale

Skills to build:

  • Python (pandas, scikit-learn, PyTorch or TensorFlow)
  • SQL -- you'll write more SQL than you expect
  • Basic computer vision (image classification, object detection)
  • Understanding of recommendation systems (collaborative filtering, content-based)
  • Git, Docker, basic MLOps
  • Fashion domain knowledge -- learn the supply chain, learn sizing, learn merchandising vocabulary

Target companies:

  • Stitch Fix (their algo team is legendary for entry-level ML)
  • The Yes (AI-first fashion platform)
  • Zalando (Berlin, but huge ML operation)
  • Amazon Fashion (massive team, good training ground)
  • Shopify (ML for fashion merchants)
  • EDITED (fashion data analytics)

Salary range: $85,000 - $130,000 (higher end at FAANG-adjacent companies)

Year 1-3: Building Expertise

Titles: ML Engineer, Data Scientist II, Applied Scientist

What you're actually doing:

  • Owning a model end-to-end (training, deployment, monitoring)
  • Building recommendation systems that serve millions of users
  • Working on computer vision -- visual search, style matching, virtual try-on
  • Collaborating with product and design teams
  • Mentoring interns and junior engineers

Skills to build:

  • Deep learning (CNNs for fashion image analysis, transformers for NLP on product descriptions)
  • Recommendation system architectures (two-tower models, ranking systems)
  • Computer vision specialization (pose estimation, garment segmentation, GANs for try-on)
  • MLOps maturity (model monitoring, A/B testing frameworks, feature stores)
  • System design for ML at scale
  • Communication -- translating model outputs into business decisions

Target companies:

  • Stitch Fix, Zalando, ASOS (fashion-first ML teams)
  • Pinterest (visual discovery, huge fashion vertical)
  • Snap (AR try-on, body tracking)
  • Google (Shopping ML, Lens for fashion)
  • Meta (AR/VR fashion applications)

Salary range: $130,000 - $200,000 (total comp with equity can be $180K-$280K at big tech)

Year 3-5: Senior Individual Contributor

Titles: Senior ML Engineer, Senior Data Scientist, Staff Engineer (early)

What you're actually doing:

  • Defining the ML strategy for your product area
  • Building novel architectures -- maybe a new approach to body measurement from photos
  • Leading technical design reviews
  • Publishing research (if you want to -- helps credibility but isn't required)
  • Making build-vs-buy decisions on ML infrastructure

Skills to build:

  • ML system architecture at scale
  • Research awareness -- reading papers, adapting SOTA to fashion problems
  • Leadership without authority (influencing roadmaps, mentoring)
  • Business metrics -- tying ML improvements to revenue
  • Generative AI applications (diffusion models for design, LLMs for styling)

Target companies:

  • Any of the above, plus:
  • Runway ML (generative AI for fashion content)
  • CLO Virtual Fashion (3D garment simulation)
  • Heuritech (trend forecasting with CV)
  • Your own startup (this is where many people branch off)

Salary range: $180,000 - $280,000 base; total comp $250K-$450K at big tech

Year 5-10: Leadership / Deep IC

Titles: Staff ML Engineer, Principal Engineer, Head of AI, VP Engineering (ML)

What you're actually doing (two options):

IC track: Solving the hardest problems. Maybe you're the person who figures out how to do photorealistic virtual try-on from a single photo. You define technical direction for entire ML orgs.

Management track: Running an ML team of 10-50 engineers. Setting the AI strategy for the company. Presenting to the board about how ML drives revenue.

Skills to build:

  • Organizational leadership or deep technical mastery (pick one, be great at it)
  • Fundraising / business development (if startup path)
  • Industry visibility -- conference talks, publications, advisory roles
  • Cross-functional leadership -- working with C-suite on strategy

Target companies:

  • Fashion tech companies that need to build out ML: Farfetch, Mytheresa, NET-A-PORTER
  • Start your own company (common exit at this level)
  • Advisory roles at multiple fashion tech startups
  • Big tech fashion verticals (Google, Amazon, Meta)

Salary range: $250,000 - $400,000 base; total comp $400K-$800K+ at big tech; equity upside if startup

Fast Track Moves

  • Publish a paper or open-source project in fashion AI -- instant credibility
  • Build a side project (fashion image classifier, style recommender) and put it on GitHub
  • Get into Stitch Fix's algo team -- it's basically a fashion ML PhD program that pays well
  • Learn computer vision deeply -- it's the scarcest skill in fashion tech
  • Attend and present at RecSys, CVPR, or KDD with fashion applications

Pivot Options

  • Move to Product (Path 3) -- ML engineers who understand the tech make incredible PMs
  • Move to Data & Analytics (Path 4) -- easier lateral, especially if you want less engineering
  • Start a company -- this path produces the most fashion tech founders

Path 2: Creative-Technical (Digital Fashion Designer)

This is the path for people who want to make things look beautiful AND understand the tech behind it. You're designing clothes that might never exist physically, building AR filters, creating digital fashion for games and virtual worlds.

Year 0-1: Getting Your Foot In

Titles: 3D Design Assistant, Junior Digital Designer, Digital Fashion Intern, 3D Artist (Fashion)

What you're actually doing:

  • Creating 3D garment models in CLO3D or Browzwear
  • Rendering product images for e-commerce
  • Assisting senior designers with digital samples
  • Learning fabric simulation and material properties
  • Maybe creating simple AR filters or Snapchat lenses

Skills to build:

  • CLO3D or Browzwear (industry standard for digital garments)
  • Blender or Maya (3D modeling fundamentals)
  • Adobe Creative Suite (Photoshop, Illustrator -- still essential)
  • Basic understanding of textiles and garment construction
  • Substance Painter/Designer for materials and textures
  • Photography and lighting for product rendering

Target companies:

  • CLO Virtual Fashion (maker of CLO3D -- they hire designers who use their tool)
  • The Fabricant (digital fashion house -- pioneer in the space)
  • DressX (digital fashion marketplace)
  • PVH Corp (Calvin Klein, Tommy Hilfiger -- big on 3D design)
  • Adidas (huge digital design operation)
  • Nike (same -- they're all-in on 3D)

Salary range: $55,000 - $80,000 (lower than ML but the ceiling grows fast)

Year 1-3: Building Your Portfolio

Titles: Digital Fashion Designer, 3D Designer, Creative Technologist

What you're actually doing:

  • Designing digital-only collections
  • Creating virtual try-on experiences
  • Building digital twins of physical garments for e-commerce
  • Collaborating with developers on AR/VR fashion experiences
  • Maybe selling NFT fashion or designing for gaming platforms

Skills to build:

  • Advanced 3D garment simulation (realistic draping, physics)
  • Unreal Engine or Unity (for real-time fashion experiences)
  • AR development basics (Spark AR, Lens Studio)
  • Motion design and animation
  • Understanding of game engines for virtual fashion
  • Basic coding (JavaScript, Python) -- makes you way more versatile
  • UX thinking -- how people interact with digital fashion

Target companies:

  • The Fabricant, DressX (digital-native fashion)
  • Gucci, Balenciaga, Burberry (luxury brands investing heavily in digital)
  • Epic Games / Fortnite (fashion collabs are huge)
  • Roblox (digital fashion marketplace)
  • Snap (AR fashion experiences)
  • Zero10 (AR try-on platform)

Salary range: $80,000 - $130,000

Year 3-5: Leading Creative Vision

Titles: Senior Digital Fashion Designer, Lead Creative Technologist, Art Director (Digital)

What you're actually doing:

  • Defining the visual language for digital fashion at your company
  • Leading a small team of designers
  • Collaborating with engineering on new rendering techniques
  • Working with brands on digital fashion partnerships
  • Speaking at conferences, building your personal brand

Skills to build:

  • Team leadership and creative direction
  • Brand strategy for digital fashion
  • Understanding of generative AI tools (Midjourney, Stable Diffusion) and how they fit your workflow
  • Business acumen -- understanding revenue models for digital fashion
  • Cross-platform thinking (web, mobile, AR, VR, gaming)

Target companies:

  • Any major luxury brand (they're all building digital teams)
  • Apple (Vision Pro fashion experiences)
  • Meta (VR fashion)
  • Fashion tech startups (often as a co-founder or first creative hire)

Salary range: $120,000 - $180,000

Year 5-10: Creative Leadership

Titles: Head of Digital Design, Creative Director (Digital), VP Creative

What you're actually doing:

  • Setting creative strategy for all digital fashion output
  • Hiring and building a digital design team
  • Partnering with C-suite on the company's digital fashion vision
  • Advisory roles, board seats, industry influence
  • Maybe launching your own digital fashion house

Skills to build:

  • Executive communication and stakeholder management
  • P&L understanding -- owning a budget
  • Industry thought leadership
  • Business development and partnerships
  • Fundraising (if starting your own thing)

Target companies:

  • Head of digital at major fashion houses (LVMH group, Kering group)
  • Creative leadership at fashion tech companies
  • Your own digital fashion studio
  • Consulting for brands entering digital fashion

Salary range: $160,000 - $300,000+ (equity and advisory fees can push much higher)

Fast Track Moves

  • Create viral digital fashion content on social media -- this is how The Fabricant's founders got noticed
  • Win a digital fashion competition (CLO3D runs them regularly)
  • Collaborate with a known brand on a digital collection, even speculatively
  • Learn Unreal Engine -- the gap between "CLO3D designer" and "real-time fashion in Unreal" is massive in market value
  • Build an AR fashion filter that goes viral on Instagram or Snapchat

Pivot Options

  • Move to Product (Path 3) -- creative technologists who understand users are great PMs
  • Move to Sustainability (Path 5) -- digital fashion IS sustainability (no physical waste)
  • Move to ML/AI (Path 1) -- if you pick up the coding skills, your design intuition is a superpower

Path 3: Product Management (Fashion Tech PM)

You're the person who decides what gets built. In fashion tech, that means understanding both the technology and the fashion consumer deeply enough to make the right bets.

Year 0-1: Getting Your Foot In

Titles: Associate Product Manager, Product Analyst, Junior PM, Business Analyst (Product team)

What you're actually doing:

  • Writing user stories and acceptance criteria
  • Analyzing product metrics (conversion rates, engagement, retention)
  • Running user research sessions with shoppers
  • Supporting senior PMs on feature launches
  • Learning the codebase well enough to talk to engineers without embarrassing yourself

Skills to build:

  • SQL and basic data analysis (you'll live in dashboards)
  • User research methods (interviews, surveys, usability testing)
  • Wireframing (Figma -- just enough to communicate ideas)
  • A/B testing methodology
  • Agile/Scrum (yes, it's boring but you need to know it)
  • Fashion e-commerce fundamentals -- conversion funnels, merchandising, sizing

Target companies:

  • Stitch Fix (APM program is competitive but excellent)
  • Shopify (fashion merchant tools)
  • Nordstrom, Macy's (digital transformation teams)
  • ThredUp, Poshmark, The RealReal (resale platforms)
  • Rent the Runway (rental model is a fascinating PM challenge)
  • SSENSE, Farfetch (luxury e-commerce)

Salary range: $80,000 - $120,000

Year 1-3: Owning Features

Titles: Product Manager, PM II

What you're actually doing:

  • Owning a product area (search, recommendations, checkout, try-on)
  • Defining and tracking OKRs
  • Making trade-off decisions daily
  • Running sprint planning and roadmap reviews
  • Building relationships with engineering, design, data science, and merchandising

Skills to build:

  • Roadmap planning and prioritization frameworks
  • Technical depth in your area (if you own recommendations, understand how the ML works)
  • Stakeholder management -- fashion companies have complex politics
  • Market analysis -- what competitors are doing, what's emerging
  • Experimentation at scale
  • Fashion trend awareness -- you need to know what customers want before they do

Target companies:

  • Any of the above, plus:
  • Pinterest (fashion discovery features)
  • Instagram Shopping (fashion is their biggest vertical)
  • Google Shopping (fashion-specific features)
  • Depop, Vestiaire Collective (European resale)

Salary range: $120,000 - $180,000 (total comp $150K-$250K with equity at tech companies)

Year 3-5: Senior PM / Group PM

Titles: Senior Product Manager, Group PM, Lead PM

What you're actually doing:

  • Owning a major product pillar (all of personalization, or the entire try-on experience)
  • Managing or mentoring other PMs
  • Presenting to executives on strategy and results
  • Making significant resource allocation decisions
  • Driving cross-functional initiatives

Skills to build:

  • Product strategy -- connecting company vision to quarterly roadmaps
  • People management (even as an IC, you're leading)
  • Executive communication -- concise, data-driven, persuasive
  • Industry expertise -- becoming known as a fashion tech product thinker
  • Financial modeling -- understanding unit economics of features

Target companies:

  • Senior PM roles at any major fashion tech company
  • Product lead at a growth-stage startup (True Fit, Stylitics, Vue.ai)
  • Fashion vertical PM at big tech (Amazon, Google, Meta)

Salary range: $170,000 - $250,000 base; total comp $250K-$400K at big tech

Year 5-10: Product Leadership

Titles: Director of Product, VP Product, Chief Product Officer

What you're actually doing:

  • Setting product vision for the entire company or major division
  • Building and leading a PM organization
  • Board-level presentations and strategic planning
  • Hiring, culture-building, team development
  • Industry influence -- you're shaping where fashion tech goes

Skills to build:

  • Organizational design and team building
  • Board communication and investor relations
  • M&A evaluation (should we buy that AR try-on startup?)
  • Platform thinking -- ecosystems, APIs, partnerships
  • Executive presence

Target companies:

  • CPO/VP Product at fashion tech companies
  • Head of fashion products at big tech
  • Startup co-founder (many fashion tech founders are ex-PMs)

Salary range: $220,000 - $350,000 base; total comp $350K-$600K+ with equity

Fast Track Moves

  • Ship something visible -- a feature that customers talk about accelerates your career more than anything
  • Get comfortable with ML/AI terminology -- PMs who can have deep technical conversations with data scientists are rare and valued
  • Build side projects that show product thinking (a fashion app prototype, a trend analysis tool)
  • Write publicly about fashion tech product decisions -- blog posts, Twitter threads, conference talks
  • Move to a smaller company where you own more surface area earlier

Pivot Options

  • Move to ML/AI (Path 1) -- if you have the technical background, PM experience makes you a unicorn ML leader
  • Move to Data & Analytics (Path 4) -- PMs who love data often become great analytics leaders
  • Move to Creative-Technical (Path 2) -- PMs with strong design sense can transition to creative technology roles

Path 4: Data & Analytics

You're the person who turns fashion data into decisions. Trend forecasting, demand planning, customer segmentation, pricing optimization -- it's all data.

Year 0-1: Getting Your Foot In

Titles: Fashion Data Analyst, Trend Research Assistant, Junior Analyst, Business Intelligence Analyst

What you're actually doing:

  • Building dashboards and reports (so many dashboards)
  • Analyzing sales data, customer behavior, return rates
  • Supporting merchandising teams with data-driven insights
  • Cleaning and organizing product data (taxonomy, attributes)
  • Learning how fashion businesses actually make decisions

Skills to build:

  • SQL (you'll write it in your sleep)
  • Excel/Google Sheets (fashion companies still run on spreadsheets)
  • Visualization tools (Tableau, Looker, Power BI)
  • Python basics (pandas, matplotlib)
  • Fashion business fundamentals -- margins, sell-through rates, markdown cadence
  • Statistics fundamentals -- hypothesis testing, regression, correlation

Target companies:

  • EDITED (fashion analytics platform -- great training ground)
  • Trendalytics (trend intelligence)
  • Heuritech (AI trend forecasting)
  • Any major retailer's analytics team (Nordstrom, Zara/Inditex, H&M)
  • Stitch Fix (data is literally their product)
  • McKinsey/BCG/Bain fashion practice (consulting route)

Salary range: $60,000 - $90,000

Year 1-3: Becoming the Go-To Analyst

Titles: Data Analyst II, Senior Analyst, Trend Analyst, Demand Planning Analyst

What you're actually doing:

  • Owning analytics for a business area (women's apparel, accessories, etc.)
  • Building predictive models for demand forecasting
  • Analyzing trends using social media data, search data, runway analysis
  • Presenting findings to directors and VPs
  • Building self-serve analytics tools for business teams

Skills to build:

  • Advanced analytics (time series forecasting, clustering, classification)
  • Python for data science (scikit-learn, statsmodels)
  • Data modeling and warehouse design (dbt, Snowflake)
  • Presentation skills -- telling stories with data
  • Fashion trend analysis methodology
  • NLP basics (analyzing fashion text data -- reviews, descriptions, social posts)

Target companies:

  • Senior analyst roles at any of the above
  • Fashion analytics teams at tech companies (Google Trends, Pinterest Trends)
  • Retail consulting firms (Alvanon, First Insight)
  • Luxury conglomerates (LVMH, Kering -- they have serious data teams)

Salary range: $90,000 - $140,000

Year 3-5: Leading Analytics

Titles: Lead Analyst, Analytics Manager, Trend Forecasting Lead, Head of BI

What you're actually doing:

  • Managing a team of 3-8 analysts
  • Defining the analytics roadmap and priorities
  • Building the data infrastructure strategy
  • Owning key business metrics and reporting to C-suite
  • Partnering with ML teams on productionizing analytical models

Skills to build:

  • People management and mentoring
  • Data strategy and governance
  • Advanced forecasting methods
  • Executive stakeholder management
  • Cross-functional influence -- getting buy-in for data-driven decisions
  • Cloud data platforms (AWS, GCP, Azure)

Target companies:

  • Analytics leadership at major fashion companies
  • Fashion tech startups (often first analytics hire or leader)
  • Fashion data companies (WGSN, EDITED) in leadership roles

Salary range: $140,000 - $200,000

Year 5-10: Analytics Executive

Titles: Head of Analytics, VP Data, Chief Data Officer, Chief Analytics Officer

What you're actually doing:

  • Setting the data vision for the company
  • Building and scaling a data organization (analysts, engineers, scientists)
  • Driving data-informed culture across the company
  • Board presentations on data capabilities and competitive advantage
  • M&A and partnership evaluation

Skills to build:

  • Executive leadership and organizational building
  • Data ethics and privacy (increasingly important in fashion -- body data, preferences)
  • Industry thought leadership
  • Budget management and vendor evaluation
  • AI strategy (how analytics evolves with generative AI)

Target companies:

  • CDO at fashion companies or fashion tech companies
  • VP Data at larger organizations
  • Advisory roles and board seats
  • Founding a fashion data/analytics company

Salary range: $200,000 - $350,000+ (total comp can reach $500K+ with equity)

Fast Track Moves

  • Learn fashion-specific analytics deeply -- most analysts are generalists, fashion expertise is rare
  • Build a trend forecasting model as a side project and publish the results
  • Get EDITED or WGSN certified -- shows you know the industry tools
  • Present at fashion industry conferences (not just tech conferences)
  • Master the art of the insight memo -- a two-page doc that changes a VP's mind is worth more than a 50-page report

Pivot Options

  • Move to ML/AI (Path 1) -- this is the most natural on-ramp to the ML path
  • Move to Product (Path 3) -- data-oriented PMs are highly valued
  • Move to Sustainability (Path 5) -- sustainability metrics and reporting need strong analysts

Path 5: Sustainability & Circular Economy

This path barely existed five years ago. Now it's one of the fastest-growing areas in fashion tech. You're working on resale, rental, repair, recycling, supply chain transparency, and carbon accounting.

Year 0-1: Getting Your Foot In

Titles: Sustainability Analyst, Operations Associate, Circular Economy Coordinator, Supply Chain Analyst

What you're actually doing:

  • Tracking sustainability metrics (carbon emissions, water usage, waste)
  • Supporting resale or rental operations
  • Researching materials and supply chain alternatives
  • Building reports for sustainability disclosures (ESG reporting)
  • Learning the regulatory landscape (EU textile regulations, EPR laws)

Skills to build:

  • Life cycle assessment (LCA) basics
  • Carbon accounting and GHG Protocol
  • Supply chain fundamentals
  • Data analysis (Excel, SQL, basic Python)
  • Understanding of textile science and manufacturing
  • ESG reporting frameworks (GRI, SASB, TCFD)
  • Circular economy principles (Ellen MacArthur Foundation framework)

Target companies:

  • ThredUp, The RealReal, Vestiaire Collective (resale platforms)
  • Rent the Runway (rental model)
  • Patagonia, Eileen Fisher (sustainability leaders)
  • Circular.co, Renewal Workshop (circular economy startups)
  • Sustainalytics, Higg Co (sustainability measurement)
  • Good On You (brand ratings)

Salary range: $55,000 - $80,000

Year 1-3: Building Domain Expertise

Titles: Sustainability Specialist, Program Manager, Circular Economy Manager, Impact Analyst

What you're actually doing:

  • Managing sustainability programs (take-back programs, recycling initiatives)
  • Building partnerships with recyclers, resellers, repair services
  • Leading carbon reduction strategy for product lines
  • Implementing supply chain traceability tools
  • Working with product teams on sustainable feature development

Skills to build:

  • Program management and cross-functional leadership
  • Supply chain traceability technologies (blockchain, RFID, digital product passports)
  • Regulatory expertise (EU Digital Product Passport, textile waste regulations)
  • Stakeholder engagement -- brands, NGOs, regulators
  • Impact measurement and reporting
  • Basic understanding of material science and textile recycling

Target companies:

  • Sustainability teams at major fashion groups (H&M, Inditex, LVMH, Kering)
  • Resale and circular platforms (scaling up teams)
  • Fashion tech companies building traceability tools (TextileGenesis, FibreTrace)
  • Consulting firms with sustainability practices (McKinsey Sustainability, Kearney)

Salary range: $80,000 - $130,000

Year 3-5: Leading Programs

Titles: Senior Manager, Circular Economy Lead, Director of Sustainability, Head of Impact

What you're actually doing:

  • Owning the sustainability strategy for a product line or business unit
  • Building a team (hiring analysts, coordinators, engineers)
  • Setting targets and reporting to leadership on progress
  • Representing the company at industry events and regulatory discussions
  • Evaluating technology solutions for sustainability (traceability, recycling tech)

Skills to build:

  • Strategic planning and goal setting
  • Team building and management
  • Policy and regulatory navigation
  • Technology evaluation and vendor management
  • Public speaking and thought leadership
  • Budget management

Target companies:

  • Director-level roles at fashion companies with serious sustainability commitments
  • Leadership at circular economy startups (scaling phase)
  • Fashion tech companies building sustainability tools
  • Industry organizations (Sustainable Apparel Coalition, Fashion for Good)

Salary range: $130,000 - $200,000

Year 5-10: Executive Leadership

Titles: VP Sustainability, Chief Sustainability Officer, Head of Innovation, VP Circular Economy

What you're actually doing:

  • Defining the company's sustainability vision and strategy
  • Board-level reporting and investor communications
  • Regulatory engagement and industry leadership
  • Building partnerships that reshape the business model
  • Driving innovation in materials, processes, and business models

Skills to build:

  • Executive leadership and board communication
  • Investor relations (ESG is a major investment thesis now)
  • Public policy and advocacy
  • Innovation management
  • M&A (acquiring sustainable technologies and companies)

Target companies:

  • CSO at major fashion companies
  • Leadership at fashion tech companies focused on sustainability
  • Founding a sustainability-focused fashion tech company
  • Advisory and board roles across the industry

Salary range: $180,000 - $300,000+ (equity and advisory income can push much higher)

Fast Track Moves

  • Get deeply technical on one thing -- digital product passports, textile recycling tech, or carbon accounting -- and become the person everyone calls
  • Publish research or analysis on the circular fashion economy
  • Build relationships with EU regulators -- they're driving the global agenda
  • Work at a resale platform early -- ThredUp, The RealReal, Vestiaire -- these are the proving grounds
  • Learn enough code to prototype sustainability tools -- even simple calculators or dashboards

Pivot Options

  • Move to Product (Path 3) -- sustainability PMs are increasingly in demand
  • Move to Data & Analytics (Path 4) -- sustainability measurement is fundamentally an analytics problem
  • Move to ML/AI (Path 1) -- supply chain optimization and material science are ML-heavy

The Honest Truth About Getting Started

No matter which path you pick, here's what actually matters in the first two years:

  1. Pick a lane, but stay curious. You need depth to get hired, but breadth to get promoted.
  2. Build things. A portfolio project beats a certification every time. Build a fashion recommendation engine, design a digital collection, create a sustainability dashboard -- whatever matches your path.
  3. Learn the fashion business. Tech people who don't understand fashion make bad products. Read The Business of Fashion. Follow fashion weeks. Understand why a $50 t-shirt and a $500 t-shirt exist in the same market.
  4. Network in both worlds. Go to fashion events AND tech meetups. The people who bridge both communities are rare and valuable.
  5. Don't wait for the perfect role. The first fashion tech job is the hardest to get. Take adjacent roles and move laterally. A data analyst at Nordstrom can become an ML engineer at a fashion startup within two years.

The industry is growing fast enough that there's room for everyone. The question isn't whether fashion tech will be big -- it already is. The question is where you want to be when it gets bigger.
