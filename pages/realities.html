Section Eleven

Industry Realities

Career Positioning

  • Your fashion knowledge is your moat. Most engineers at fashion tech companies don't understand fashion. If you do, that's not a weakness in tech -- it's a competitive advantage. Don't try to out-engineer engineers. Be the person who connects both worlds.
  • You don't need a CS degree. Creative technologist, product manager, trend analyst, digital merchandiser -- all value fashion knowledge heavily.

What's Actually Growing vs Overhyped

Growing fast

  • Resale/circular economy tech (regulation-driven)
  • Size/fit technology
  • Supply chain transparency
  • AI product tagging

Steady and real

  • E-commerce personalization
  • Trend analytics
  • 3D design tools

Overhyped or cooling

  • NFT fashion (mostly dead)
  • Metaverse fashion (pivoting to gaming)
  • Blockchain for fashion (limited adoption)

Emerging

  • AI-generated designs
  • On-demand manufacturing
  • Fashion copilots for designers

Funding Landscape

  • Sustainability/circular economy: Heavy VC interest due to EU regulations
  • AI-powered commerce: Still funded, especially personalization and visual search
  • Resale platforms: Consolidating. Harder for new entrants
  • Digital fashion/3D: Funding cooled from 2022 peak but real use cases sustain interest

Hiring Seasonality

Fashion tech hiring follows the fashion calendar:

  • Peak hiring: Jan - Mar (post-holiday, budget refresh) and Aug - Sep (pre-holiday ramp)
  • Slow: Jun - Jul and Nov - Dec
  • Luxury brands (LVMH, Kering) have longer, more formal processes. Startups move in 2 weeks
